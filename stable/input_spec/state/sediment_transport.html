Sediment Transport#

Most of the below are rates of suspension / fallout used in sediment transport. The biomass evaluator lets the others be a function of biomass.

Biomass#

src/physics/ats/src/pks/transport/constitutive_relations/sediment_transport/biomass_evaluator.hh

This biomass model evaluates evolution of biomass as a function of “mean sea level”. There are 2 models implemented.

Model 1:

\[\begin{split}B = \left\{\begin{array}{ll} B_{max}\frac{(z_{max} - z_b)}{(z_{max} - z_{min})} &\qquad\mbox{if}\qquad z_{min}<z_b<z_{max} \\ 0 &\qquad\mbox{otherwise} \end{array}\right.\end{split}\]

Model 2:

\[\begin{split}B = \left\{\begin{array}{ll} B_{max} &\qquad\mbox{if}\qquad z_b\ge z_{max} \\ B_{max} \frac{(z_b - z_{min})}{(z_{max} - z_{min})} &\qquad\mbox{if}\qquad z_{min}<z_b<z_{max} \\ 0 &\qquad\mbox{otherwise} \end{array}\right.\end{split}\]

where \(B_{max}\) is a maximum biomass, \(z_b = h - MSL\), MSL is a mean sea level, and \(h\) is a surface elevation.

“evaluator type” = “biomass”

evaluator-biomass-spec

  • “Bmax” [double] 2000.0

  • “zmax” [double] 0.8

  • “zmin” [double] 0.0

DEPENDENCIES:

  • “mean sea level” DOMAIN-mean_sea_level

  • “elevation” DOMAIN-elevation

Organic Matter#

src/physics/ats/src/pks/transport/constitutive_relations/sediment_transport/organic_matter_evaluator.hh

The organic evaluator gets the organic matter deposition rates.

..math:

Q_{db} = Q_{db_0} \frac{B}{B_{max}}

where \(Q_{db_0}\) is a typical deposition rate, empirical coefficient with the dimensions of [L/T], which is derived empirically from field measurements, \(B\) is the current biomass, \(B_{max}\) is the maximum value of the biomass.

“evaluator type” = “organic matter rate”

evaluator-organic-matter-rate-spec

  • “empirical coefficient” [double] 9.5129e-11

  • “maximum biomass” [double] 2000

DEPENDENCIES:

  • “biomass” DOMAIN-biomass

Erosion#

src/physics/ats/src/pks/transport/constitutive_relations/sediment_transport/erosion_evaluator.hh

The erosion evaluator gets the erosion rates. We evaluate the erosion flux, \(Q_e\), by way of a relationship which can be applied when the bed properties are relatively uniform over the depth and the bed is consolidated [e.g., Mehta, 1984], namely,

\[\begin{split}Q_e = \left\{\begin{array}{ll} Q_{e_0}\left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau_e} - 1\right) &\qquad\mbox{if} \qquad \tau_0>\tau_e \\ 0 &\qquad\mbox{if} \qquad \tau_0\le\tau_e \end{array} \right.\end{split}\]

where \(Q_{e_0}\) is an empirical coefficient, \(\tau_e\) is a critical shear stress

“evaluator type” = “erosion rate”

evaluator-erosion-rate-spec

  • “critical shear stress” [double] 0.4

  • “empirical coefficient” [double] 3.0e-4

  • “drag coefficient” [double] 0.02

  • “specific weight of water” [double] 9806.0

DEPENDENCIES:

  • “velocity” DOMAIN-velocity

Settlement#

src/physics/ats/src/pks/transport/constitutive_relations/sediment_transport/settlement_evaluator.hh

The settlement evaluator gets the settlement rates. We estimate the deposition due to settling, Q_ds which is mainly due to the formation and breakup of flocs, by way of the formulation proposed by Einstein and Krone [1962] who assumed that most of the particles settled in flocs as long as near bed shear stresses are small enough to prevent their breaking up, namely,

\[\begin{split}Q_ds = \left\{\begin{array}{ll} \rho_s w_s min(C, 0.5)\left(1-\frac{\tau_0}{\tau_d}\right) &\qquad\mbox{if} \qquad \tau_0<\tau_d \\ 0 &\qquad\mbox{if} \qquad \tau_0\ge\tau_d \end{array} \right.\end{split}\]

where \(\rho_s\) is the sediment density, \(w_s\) is the settling velocity which depends on the size of sediment flocs, \(\tau_d\) is a critical shear stress, C in a sediment molar ratio.

“evaluator type” = “settlement rate”

evaluator-settlement-rate-spec

  • “critical shear stress” [double] 0.1

  • “settling velocity” [double] 0.0001

  • “drag coefficient” [double] 0.02

  • “specific weight of water” [double] 9806.0

  • “velocity” DOMAIN-velocity

  • “sediment” DOMAIN-sediment
